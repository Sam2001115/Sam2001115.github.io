PROJECT: CLEANING_ROVER

01. Objective

To engineer an autonomous room-cleaning vehicle that utilizes a brush-based system for debris collection, focusing on high-efficiency mechanical movement and wireless user control.

02. Technology Used

ESP32 DevKit V1 L298N Motor Driver HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Sensors 130 DC Motors 3D Printed Gearsets 11.1V Li-ion Battery

03. The Challenge

The primary hurdle was the 130 DC motors. While they run at a high 11,500 RPM, they provide a very low native torque of only 15 g·cm. This was insufficient to move the 3D printed chassis and dust chamber. Additionally, the HC-SR04 sensors require 5V for peak performance, which exceeds the ESP32's 3.3V input tolerance, risking hardware failure.

04. Resolution

I engineered a custom 5:1 gear reduction mechanism (12-tooth pinion to 60-tooth gear) to multiply the output torque while reducing speed to a manageable 2,300 RPM.

// Torque Calculation
Native: 15 g·cm
Ratio: 5:1
Result: 75 g·cm Stall Torque

For logic safety, I implemented a voltage divider circuit (1kΩ and 2kΩ resistors) on the sensor Echo pins to safely step down the 5V signal to 3.33V, protecting the ESP32 internal registers.

The control system was resolved by hosting a WiFi Web Server directly on the ESP32, allowing real-time manual override via a web page alongside the sensor-driven autonomous mode.

05. Results

✅ The rover now climbs carpeted areas with ease and can be controlled remotely via WiFi. Stall torque increased by 5×, enabling the robot to carry a full dustbin without stalling. The voltage divider has protected the ESP32 from over-voltage for over 50 hours of runtime.
