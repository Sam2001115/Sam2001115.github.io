Autonomous Cleaning Rover

A mechanical and electronic study in torque multiplication and WiFi-enabled autonomous navigation.

Objective

Engineering an efficient floor-cleaning method utilizing a custom-designed brush system and a high-torque 3D-printed chassis.

Strategy

Solving low-torque motor limitations through a 5:1 gear reduction and implementing a dual-mode ESP32 control architecture.

Hardware Layer

The rover utilizes 130 DC motors running at 11,500 RPM. Given their low native torque (15 g·cm), I designed a 5:1 gear reduction system (12-tooth to 60-tooth) to increase output torque to 11,500 g·cm (at reduced RPM), enabling the rover to move its own weight and the dust chamber effectively.

Mechanical Math:
11500 RPM / 5 = 2300 RPM Final Speed
15 g·cm x 5 = 75 g·cm Stall Torque per motor

Power management is handled by an L298N driver, converting an 11.1V 1800mAh Lithium battery source to power both the motors and the 3.3V logic of the ESP32.

Intelligence Layer

I utilized the ESP32 DevKit V1 for its dual-core processing and integrated WiFi. The rover features a custom manual control mode via a hosted web server and an autonomous mode driven by four HC-SR04 Ultrasonic sensors.

To ensure pin safety while maximizing sensor range, I implemented a voltage divider (1kΩ/2kΩ) on the 5V Echo signals to match the ESP32’s 3.3V input tolerance.
